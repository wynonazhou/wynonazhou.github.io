FSU Virtual ID: Research Study

The problem

As college students, our student id cards are an essential item. Unfortunately, like many of our essential items, they are easy to get lost. If students were to lose their ids,they would be locked out of performing their essentials daily tasks. Some of these tasks include accessing facilities, purchasing food, and renting out books and supplies.

In order to relieve students of these easy to lose cards, we proposed the idea of Florida State University moving away from the physical onto the digital. Every student nowadays seems to have a smart mobile device. If a student could use their phone instead of having to carry around the loose id card, it could say alot of pain for both students and faculty at the college. However, it is essential to understand if this is truly a need for the college's student body.


The solution

Working alongside four team members, we ran a semester-long study on FSU students in different majors, through in-person interviews and surveys, on their interests in having a student id app on their mobile devices. We were able to find out that students, or at least the portion of students that we sampled, were interested in a mobile-based college id. We were sadly unable to complete a prototype of the app. However, we were able to find out that there were faculty members on campus, such as the library IT department, that were attempting to produce an app that utilized technology to replace id cards.
