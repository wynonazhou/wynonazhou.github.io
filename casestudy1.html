Kanban Organization Board - The Children's Campaign

The problem

For this project, I was tasked alongside my team members on the tech team at the Children's Campaign to find a solution for our in-office lack of communication. Throughout my year at the Children's Campaign, I observed or was affected by this issue. Some examples of issues that arose due to these communication issues included numerous missed deadlines, reported negative tension between teams, and stagnation for new implementations(ex: improved training guides, new technology purchases, etc.). The team leads from the communication, policy, office management, and tech teams agreed that the way we were handling the way we were all communicating needed to be improved immediately. The team leads decided to place the responsibilities to find a solution upon the tech team.


The solution

Through a meeting with my fellow interns and our manager, we decided that the best solution for this problem was to utilize Kanban. At the time, the tech team was using Asana, which is a task management tool that was introduced to us through our team's manager, to manage our projects and daily ticket requests. Due to past rejection with other technology systems, I suggested a kanban board system that I discovered through my previous online research. After agreeing upon this plan, I introduced it to the other team leads with fortunate approval. We were also lucky to have an available room for the kanban boards. After a few trials, all of the kanban boards were finally implemented (as seen in the images below).
